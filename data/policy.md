# Mumzworld Customer Care Policy (Internal) — Mock for Assessment

> This document is a realistic *mock* internal policy for the take-home assessment.
> The AI assistant must cite clause numbers (e.g., `2.3`) when grounding replies.

## 1. Scope & Definitions

1.1 This policy covers **returns**, **refunds**, **exchanges**, **delivery expectations**, and **escalation triggers** for customer support email handling.  
1.2 “Delivery date” refers to the date the order status is marked **Delivered** by the carrier.  
1.3 “Business days” means Monday–Friday excluding public holidays in the destination country.

## 2. Returns

2.1 **Return window**: Customers may request a return within **7 days** from the delivery date.  
2.2 **Condition requirements**: Items must be **unused**, **unwashed**, and in **original packaging** with all accessories, manuals, and tags included.  
2.3 **Return eligibility checks**: If an item shows signs of use, missing parts, or damaged packaging due to handling, the return may be rejected or escalated for review.  
2.4 **Information required from customer**: Order number, item name/SKU, reason for return, and clear photos if the issue relates to damage/wrong item.  
2.5 **Free home pickup (UAE)**: Free home pickup can be scheduled within **3 calendar days** after the return is approved, subject to courier availability.  
2.6 **Free home pickup (KSA)**: Free home pickup can be scheduled within **5 calendar days** after the return is approved, subject to courier availability.

## 3. Refunds

3.1 **Refund method**: Refunds are processed to the **original payment method** used at checkout whenever possible.  
3.2 **Refund timeline**: Refunds are processed within **5–7 business days** after the return is received and approved by the warehouse/quality check.  
3.3 **Refund status updates**: Customers are notified by email once the refund is initiated (processing time with banks may vary).  
3.4 **No backdating**: Agents must not promise refunds to be backdated or applied to a previous statement period. (See also 6.2.)

## 4. Exchanges

4.1 **Same value only**: Exchanges are allowed only for items of the **same value** (same price tier) unless a manager approves an exception.  
4.2 **Subject to availability**: Exchanges are subject to stock availability at the time of processing.  
4.3 **Exchange process**: Exchanges are handled after eligibility is confirmed and may require a return pickup before dispatching the replacement.

## 5. Delivery Expectations & Delays

5.1 **Standard delivery (UAE)**: Typical delivery is **1–3 days** from dispatch, depending on the destination city and courier constraints.  
5.2 **Standard delivery (KSA)**: Typical delivery is **3–5 days** from dispatch, depending on the destination city and courier constraints.  
5.3 **Delay communication**: If delays occur, updates should be communicated via **email** using the latest courier status and internal tracking notes.  
5.4 **No exact timestamps**: Agents must not promise exact delivery timestamps (e.g., “today at 3 PM”). (See also 6.1.)

## 6. What Agents Cannot Promise

6.1 **Exact delivery timestamps**: Agents can provide date ranges and courier status, but must not guarantee an exact time.  
6.2 **Backdated refunds**: Agents cannot promise refunds to be backdated or reflected in a specific past billing cycle.  
6.3 **Policy exceptions without approval**: Agents must not promise exceptions (e.g., return outside window, exchange to different value) without **manager approval**.

## 7. Escalation Triggers

7.1 **Health-critical products**: Any issue involving products that may be health-critical (e.g., baby formula, allergy-related items, safety recalls) must be escalated.  
7.2 **High-value orders**: Orders over **AED 500** should be escalated to a senior agent for review and response.  
7.3 **Repeated delivery failures**: If there are **3 or more failed delivery attempts**, escalate for carrier investigation and customer resolution.
